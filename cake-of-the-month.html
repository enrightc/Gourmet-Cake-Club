Chocolate Nutella Cake

Indulge in the rich and decadent flavours of our featured cake this month

Jump to Recipe →

Prep time: 40 mins Cook time: 40 mins
Servings: 12 Skill level: Easy

Decadence Defined: Unveiling the Nutella Delight Cake

Indulge in the sublime world of our Nutella Delight Cake – a harmonious blend of rich, moist chocolate layers and velvety Nutella buttercream. This opulent dessert promises an unrivaled experience that invites all cake enthusiasts to savour.

What sets our Nutella chocolate cake apart as the pinnacle of perfection? At its core, this masterpiece is an ode to Nutella, generously woven into every element. From the luxurious cake batter to the heavenly Swiss meringue frosting, each bite is a symphony of hazelnut-chocolate decadence, ensuring a truly extraordinary treat for your senses.

Moreover, the simplicity of crafting this cake sets it apart from other layer cakes. With each of the three layers baked in its dedicated cake pan, the process eliminates the complexity of slicing cake rounds into thinner portions. Say goodbye to the challenges of achieving even shapes; this cake offers a hassle-free experience. Whether you're a baking novice or an experienced pro, this is the quintessential layer cake that caters to all levels of expertise!

Ingredients

For the Cake

  • 2 cups (260 g) all-purpose flour
  • ⅔ cup (57 g) unsweetened cocoa powder, plus more for dusting the pans
  • 2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ¾ cup (170 g) unsalted butter, room temperature
  • 1 ¾ cups (350 g) granulated sugar
  • ⅓ cup (113 g) Nutella
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups (480 ml) buttermilk, room temperature - Note 1

For the Frosting

  • 6 large egg whites
  • 1 cup (200 g) granulated sugar
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 2 ½ cups (565 g) unsalted butter, cut into pieces at room temperature
  • ¾ cup (296 g) Nutella
  • chocolate shavings, optional

Recipe

Preparation:

  1. Preheat your oven to 177°C. Grease three 20 cm round pans with nonstick spray and line the bottoms with parchment paper cut into circles. Spray the parchment paper with nonstick spray and lightly dust the pans with cocoa powder. Tap out any excess and set aside.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, cocoa powder, baking soda, baking powder, and salt. Set aside.
  3. Using a stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment or a handheld electric mixer, beat the butter and sugar on medium-low speed until light and fluffy. Mix in the Nutella until well combined. Add the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition. Scrape down the sides of the bowl as needed and beat in the vanilla.
  4. With the mixer on low speed, gradually add half of the flour mixture, followed by the buttermilk, and then the remaining flour mixture. Beat just until combined; the batter will be thick.
  5. Divide the batter evenly between the prepared pans, spreading it to the edges with an offset spatula. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out with only a few dry crumbs.
  6. Cool the cakes in the pans for 10 minutes. Invert them onto a wire rack, peel off the parchment paper, and let the cakes cool completely.

For the Frosting:

  1. In a stand mixer bowl, combine egg whites, sugar, and salt. Place the bowl over simmering water, whisking continuously until the mixture reaches 65°C and the sugar is fully dissolved.
  2. Transfer the bowl to the stand mixer, fit it with the whisk attachment, and beat on medium speed until the mixture resembles shaving cream and cools slightly (1-2 minutes).
  3. Switch to the paddle attachment and gradually add butter, one tablespoon at a time, until smooth and creamy. Scrape down the sides of the bowl and beat on medium-high until light and fluffy (about 30 seconds). Don't worry if it looks curdled; it will come together.
  4. Add Nutella and continue mixing until incorporated. If the frosting is too thick, mix on medium-low to slightly deflate the egg whites and thin it out.

Assembly:

  1. Place one cake layer on a serving platter, spread 1 cup of frosting to the edges. Repeat with the second layer, topping it with 1 cup of frosting. Add the third layer and cover the top and sides of the cake with frosting.
  2. Sprinkle with chocolate shavings if desired. Enjoy this indulgent Nutella chocolate cake!

Notes

Tips for Baking Success

  1. Buttermilk Magic: Buttermilk plays a crucial role in this recipe, particularly due to the presence of baking soda. The acidity in buttermilk neutralises the baking soda, eliminating any lingering off-flavours. If you find yourself without buttermilk, a quick substitute can be made by adding 1 tablespoon of lemon juice or vinegar to a 1-cup liquid measuring cup. Fill the cup with whole milk, stir, and let it sit for 5 minutes before use. Repeat this process for every cup of buttermilk required in the recipe.
  2. Handle with Care: This cake boasts a soft and delicate texture. For optimal results, allow the layers to cool and chill before applying the frosting. Additionally, chilling the frosted cake before slicing ensures clean and neat portions.
  3. Cocoa Powder Coating: Prevent the cake from sticking to the pans by dusting the insides with cocoa powder. Unlike flour, cocoa powder seamlessly blends with the dark cake color, ensuring a flawless appearance.
  4. Embrace Room Temperature: Ensure all ingredients are at room temperature. Cold eggs can be challenging to blend into cake batter without overworking it, and cold buttermilk may cause the butter to resolidify, leading to batter curdling.
  5. Precision Weighing: For the finest results, consider weighing your ingredients. Small kitchen scales are a relatively inexpensive yet invaluable tool for achieving accuracy in your baking endeavors.

Planning ahead

  1. Room Temperature Bliss: Keep the frosted cake in a cake keeper at room temperature for up to 3 days or refrigerate for up to 5 days. Bring it to room temperature before serving.
  2. Frozen Delight: Freeze the frosted cake for up to 2 months. Thaw it overnight in the refrigerator and bring it to room temperature just before serving.
  3. Layer Preparation: Alternatively, wrap the baked and cooled cake layers in plastic wrap and store them in the refrigerator for up to 2 days before frosting and serving.
  4. Frosting Freedom: Prepare the frosting one day in advance, keeping it refrigerated in an airtight container. Bring it to room temperature and re-whip before using.
  5. Frozen Flavour: The frosting can also be frozen for up to 2 months. Thaw it in the refrigerator overnight, bring it to room temperature, and re-whip before using for a delightful frozen treat.
